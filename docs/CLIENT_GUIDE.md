# What Sable Delivers — A Guide for Clients

**Audience: you, the client** (e.g. TIG, SolStitch). This is a plain-language guide to what Sable does for your community and how to read your portal. It deliberately avoids internal jargon. If anything here doesn't match what you see, tell your Sable contact — the docs should always match the product.

> **One sentence:** Sable runs and grows your crypto community — content, engagement, community-health intelligence, and moderation — and gives you a portal that shows the work delivered and its impact, with every number honestly labeled as *measured* or *interpreted*.

---

## Your portal (`/client` at sable.tools)

You log in to a single page scoped to **your** community. You never see other clients, and you never see Sable's internal machinery (sales notes, costs, operator identities, raw task logs). What you see is organized top-to-bottom as a story:

1. **Executive Summary** — the state of your community right now, what changed, the top risk, and the next move.
2. **This Week** — the current recommendations, why they matter, and the evidence behind them.
3. **Proof of Value** — completed work, its observed outcomes, and an honest "what if you stopped?" view.
4. **Work Delivered** — the scale of the work the team put in for you this period.
5. **Details** — the deeper metrics, journeys, and content performance, for when you want to dig in.

## How to read each piece

### Community health grade + metrics
You get an overall **health grade** (A–F) and a set of community metrics — engagement, unique mentioners, bot-reply rate, sentiment, language/culture signals, and more.

- **Metrics are measured** from real data (with methodology caveats noted — e.g. which window, which source).
- **The letter grade is interpretive** — it's an AI-and-analyst judgment that rolls many signals into one letter. Treat it as a directional read, not a precise score. We always label it as such.

### Strategic recommendations + the feedback loop
Each cycle you get prioritized recommendations (what to do, expected outcome, urgency, and the evidence). You can mark each one **adopted** or **skipped** right in the portal — that feedback closes the loop and shapes what we recommend next. Recommendations and their rankings are **interpretive** (our judgment), and labeled that way.

### Content performance
Posts and clips we produced for you, with their real performance and format intelligence — what's landing, what isn't. Performance numbers are **measured**; "this format tends to do better" reads are **derived** from those measurements.

### Work Delivered
This card shows the scale of work the team put in for you — so renewal and value conversations rest on something concrete. Read the labels carefully, because the two halves have different reliability:

- **Replies delivered — *measured*.** A real count of replies our operators posted on your behalf, captured at post time. This is a hard number.
- **Coverage hours & communities covered — *self-reported / interpretive*.** These come from operators declaring when they were on a moderating slot and which chats they watched. They are an honest log of effort, **not a measured metric** — we never present them as precise, and we never show you operator identities or raw logs (only totals).

### Proof of Value ("what if you stopped?")
A directional, counterfactual view of what your community trajectory would look like without the work. It is **interpretive and modeled** — useful for framing, never a guarantee. We lead value conversations on hard, attributable numbers (replies delivered, completed outcomes, follower delta) and use the counterfactual only as color.

## The honesty box — measured vs interpreted

Sable labels every number so you always know how much to trust it:

| Label | Means | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Measured** | Counted directly from raw data | engagement rate, unique mentioners, bot-reply rate, **replies delivered** |
| **Derived** | Computed from measured data | content-format lift, metric deltas |
| **Interpretive** | AI/analyst judgment | health letter grade, recommendation rankings, sentiment, "what if you stopped", **coverage hours/communities** |

We will **never** quote you a guaranteed growth percentage or an impressions guarantee — reach numbers are unreliable after the fact, and we won't bill a story on them. When we say we amplify your reach, it's **measured amplification, never a promise of a number.** Replies are always **suggested by our tools and sent by a human** — nothing is auto-posted on anyone's behalf.

## Your privacy & boundaries
- Your data is **yours** — it's never shown to another client, and another client's data is never shown to you.
- You see **outcomes and deliverables**, not Sable's internal task board, costs, or operator names.
- Some capabilities (e.g. an autonomous community-manager) are **in development** and clearly labeled as not-yet-live wherever they appear.
